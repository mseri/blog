The one thing I truly value of dialogue-based podcasts, is that no-matter the topics, the free flow of the conversation often leads to unexpected and quite insightful places. This post comes from such an example, more specifically from the [Signals and Threads](https://signalsandthreads.com) episode [Why Testing is Hard and How to Fix it with Will Wilson](https://signalsandthreads.com/why-testing-is-hard-and-how-to-fix-it/).

There are important discussions about restructuring our University's governance and processes, accompanied by growing focus on students and staff wellbeing, especially in times of international turmoil and financial crunch. So it should not come as a complete surprise if my brain started picking up all the mentions of office culture that promote collaboration, honesty and mutual growth, leading to a good and agile atmosphere.

This reminded me of an older episode of the same podcast, [Solving Puzzles in Production with Liora Friedberg](https://signalsandthreads.com/solving-puzzles-in-production/).
